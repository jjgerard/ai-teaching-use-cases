Three catalogs, one bar: it has to be real

Every entry across all three catalogs describes something that was actually built, run, or trialled — a named person, course, institution, or research team, with a real source link. Proposals, frameworks, and guidance documents don't qualify anywhere on this site, no matter how sensible they sound.

Teaching Case Studies

A named person, course, or institution describing exactly what they did with generative AI in a higher-ed teaching context — collected at universities and sector bodies worldwide. No frameworks; only specific examples.

Research Case Studies

A real research project where AI was used at some stage of the actual research process — literature search, data collection, coding, analysis, hypothesis generation, drafting. Research about AI itself (benchmarking a model, studying attitudes toward AI) doesn't qualify — the study's subject has to be something else.

Reducing AI (mis)use

A strategy an educator or institution actually ran, with concrete measured evidence that student use of generative AI to complete coursework went down as a result — not a proposal, and not just an opinion that it probably helped. Where the source explains why use dropped, that's included too.

How entries get added

Anyone can suggest a case study through the submission form — one form for all three catalogs, with a field to pick which type it is. Every submission is screened against the same criteria above before it goes live; nothing is auto-published.

Who built this

This catalog and its content were built by Juliana Gerard, a lecturer in Linguistics at Ulster University. The application code was written with Claude Code (Anthropic), which was also used to source candidate case studies, screen them against the inclusion criteria, and write entries; the human work is setting those criteria, directing the research, and reviewing what actually qualifies.

The summaries, quotes-in-context, and metadata written for these catalogs are available under CC BY 4.0 — reuse them anywhere, including commercially, as long as you credit this project and link back.
